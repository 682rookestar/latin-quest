# Individual Rights and Personal Data Breach Procedures

## Rights-request procedure

1. Log the request date, scope, source and responsible controller.
2. If school-directed, notify/route to the school promptly and assist under the
   school DPA. Do not assume a parent may automatically exercise every right for a
   competent child.
3. Verify identity proportionately. Avoid collecting excessive identity evidence.
4. Clarify ambiguous requests and preserve relevant data while restriction applies.
5. Search Auth, profiles, memberships, attempts, answers, progress, badges,
   invitations, logs, support records and authorised exports where applicable.
6. Review for third-party information, legal exemptions and safeguarding concerns.
7. Respond securely within one month unless a lawful extension applies.
8. Record the decision, searches, disclosures, redactions and completion date.

Request log fields: reference, received date, person, controller, request type,
identity check, systems searched, owner, deadline, decision, response date and
follow-up/deletion evidence.

## Personal data breach procedure

### First response

1. Start an incident log immediately and record when the organisation became aware.
2. Contain the incident: revoke sessions/keys, restrict accounts, stop exports or
   isolate affected functionality without destroying evidence.
3. Inform the privacy lead and the relevant school controller without undue delay.
4. Establish affected people, data types, volume, duration, recipients and whether
   information was encrypted or otherwise protected.
5. Preserve access logs and a clear decision trail.

### Risk and notification

The controller decides whether the breach is likely to risk people's rights and
freedoms. A notifiable breach must be reported to the ICO without undue delay and,
where feasible, within 72 hours of awareness. If likely to create a high risk,
affected people must also be informed without undue delay unless an exception
applies. Latin Quest as processor must provide the school with the information and
assistance needed for that decision.

Consider identity theft, account takeover, exposure of children's educational
records, embarrassment, bullying, discrimination, safeguarding risk, permanence,
number and vulnerability of people, and ease of identification.

### Recovery

Remediate the root cause, reset credentials where needed, validate restored access
controls, monitor misuse, communicate clearly, and update the DPIA/tests. Record
every personal data breach even when notification is not required.

Incident log fields: discovery/awareness times, reporter, systems, people/records,
facts and chronology, containment, risk assessment, controller decision,
ICO/individual notifications, remediation, lessons and closure approval.
