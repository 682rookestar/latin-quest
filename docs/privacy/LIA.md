# Legitimate Interests Assessment

Status: Draft  
Applies only where Latin Quest acts as controller. A school acting as controller
must document its own lawful basis.

## Purpose test

The interests are to provide and secure an educational practice service, maintain
accurate accounts and progress, prevent abuse, diagnose faults and improve the
reliability of core functions. These are specific and lawful interests. Latin
Quest does not rely on legitimate interests for advertising or sale of pupil data.

## Necessity test

Identity, class relationship and learning records are necessary to authenticate
users, restrict teacher access, record progress and provide the requested service.
Security logs are necessary to detect attacks and investigate incidents. Less
intrusive data should be used wherever it can achieve the same result; date of
birth, address, phone number and special-category data are not requested.

## Balancing test

Pupils are children and may not freely choose a school-selected tool, so their
interests receive additional weight. They can reasonably expect a learning service
to record answers and show progress to their class teacher, but may not expect
unrelated teachers, classmates or vendors to see detailed records. Controls
therefore include class-scoped teacher access, limited leaderboard disclosure,
no advertising, restricted administrators, child-friendly transparency,
retention limits and rights-request support.

Potential harms include embarrassment from rankings, unfair conclusions from
inaccurate scores, account compromise and disclosure of educational records.
The DPIA identifies further mitigations, particularly a leaderboard opt-out,
teacher/admin MFA, audit logs and automated scoring tests.

## Outcome

Legitimate interests may be appropriate for essential controller-side security and
service administration after the open DPIA actions are addressed. It should not be
used as a blanket justification for new analytics, marketing, AI inference, or
sharing. Any new purpose requires a fresh necessity and balancing assessment.
